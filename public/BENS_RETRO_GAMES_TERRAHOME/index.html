Retro computer games from my childhood

It must have been the late nineties, perhaps early noughties when I got my second computer, a Pentium I with a solid 75Mhz clock speed. CRT monitor on top, mouse and keyboard mandatorily on a drawer shelf.

I remember the first few games I ever played on it… Dangerous Dave and Dyna Blaster (or Dyna for short).

Dangerous Dave

Dangerous Dave is a 1988 computer game by John Romero. It was developed for the Apple II and DOS as an example game to accompany his article about his GraBASIC, an Applesoft BASIC add-on, for the UpTime disk magazine.

Source: Wikipedia

Dyna Blaster

Dynablaster or Dyna Blaster is a name that has been given to the European releases of four different games of the Bomberman franchise:

  • Bomberman (TurboGrafx-16, MS-DOS, Amiga and Atari ST)
  • Bomberman (Arcade)
  • Bomberman II (NES)
  • Atomic Punk (Game Boy)

Bomberman was allegedly renamed Dyna Blaster due to the European mainstream media associating the original name with terrorist bombings.
